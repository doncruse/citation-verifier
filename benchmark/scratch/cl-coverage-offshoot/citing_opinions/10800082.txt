13        Plaintiff, an incarcerated person housed at Correctional Training Facility (“CTF”), has 
14   filed a pro se civil rights action.  For the reasons set forth below, the Court DENIES Plaintiff’s 
15   request for leave to file the proposed third amended complaint docketed at Dkt. No. 36, Dkt. No. 
16   34; and DISMISSES this action.                                        
17                               DISCUSSION                                
18        In this action, Plaintiff has alleged that CTF correctional officers Martin Magana, Oliva 
19   Castro, Don Zypel Igacio, A. Pelayo, Oscar Covarrubias, Jo-ann Crews, and Michael Hicks were 
20   deliberately indifferent to his safety when, on October 4, 2021, they housed him with a known 
21   violent prisoner, inmate Bankhead.  Dkt. Nos. 20, 21.  On December 3, 2025, the Court granted 
22   judgment on the pleadings in favor of Defendants because this case presented the “rare event” 
23   where Plaintiff’s failure to exhaust administrative remedies was clear on the face of the operative 
24   complaint and from judicially noticeable documents.  Dkt. No. 33.  Specifically, the record 
25   showed that Plaintiff had not filed any grievance challenging the decision to house him with 
26   inmate Bankhead.  Id.                                                 
27        While the motion for judgment on the pleadings was pending, Plaintiff requested leave to 
1   not accompanied by a proposed third amended complaint, as required by N.D. Cal. L.R. 10-1.  
2   Dkt. No. 33 at 11-12.  The Court further noted that it was unclear how a third amended complaint 
3   could cure the exhaustion issue as the prisoner-plaintiff is required to satisfy the exhaustion 
4   requirement prior to commencing the action.  Dkt. No. 33 at 12.   The Court’s denial of this 
5   request was without prejudice to Plaintiff refiling the request and attaching the required proposed 
6   third amended complaint.  Id.                                         
7        Plaintiff has filed a renewed request for leave to file a third amended complaint, Dkt. No. 
8   34, and filed a proposed third amended complaint, Dkt. No. 36.  The proposed third amended 
9   complaint (“TAC”) sets forth two claims.  First, the proposed TAC challenges the decision to 
10   double-cell Plaintiff at CTF Soledad II.  The proposed TAC alleges that double-celling Plaintiff at 
11   CTF violates the Eighth Amendment and state laws because prison memorandums and guidelines 
12   acknowledge that these cells are not designed for double-celling and because these cells deny 
13   Plaintiff “the minimal civilized living space in order to maintain mental and physical health and 
14   program efficiently.”  Dkt. No. 36 at 5.  The proposed TAC also alleges that double-celling at CTF 
15   violates the Equal Protection Clause because it denies Plaintiff the same amount of floor and 
16   living space as others in CDCR custody with the same work, privilege, and security level.  The 
17   proposed TAC brings this claim against defendants Allison, Maccomber, Koenig, Martinez, and 
18   Borla.  Dkt. No. 36 at 4-6.  Second, the proposed TAC repeats the Eighth Amendment claim 
19   against defendants Magana, Castro, Igacio, Pelayo, Covarrubias, Crews, and Hicks, which the 
20   Court has already dismissed for failure to exhaust.  Dkt. No. 36 at 6-7. 
21        The Court DENIES Plaintiff’s request for leave to file the proposed third amended 
22   complaint at Dkt. No. 36.  “Five factors are taken into account to assess the propriety of a motion 
23   for leave to amend: bad faith, undue delay, prejudice to the opposing party, futility of amendment, 
24   and whether the plaintiff has previously amended the complaint.”  Johnson v. Buckley, 356 F.3d 
25   1067, 1077 (9th Cir. 2004).  “It is true that leave to amend ‘shall be freely given when justice so 
26   requires,’ Fed. R. Civ. P. 15(a), and this policy is to be applied with extreme liberality.”  Morongo 
27   Band of Mission Indians v. Rose, 893 F.2d 1074, 1079 (9th Cir. 1990).  Here, granting leave to 
1   failure to exhaust and the proposed TAC does not cure the exhaustion issue.  The first claim in the 
2   proposed TAC – inadequate cell size – must be raised in a separate action because it is unrelated to 
3   the Eighth Amendment claim regarding being housed with inmate Bankhead and brought against 
4   different defendants.  Fed. R. Civ. P. 20(a)(2) provides that all persons “may be joined in one 
5   action as defendants if: (A) any right to relief is asserted against them jointly, severally, or in the 
6   alternative with respect to or arising out of the same transaction, occurrence, or series of 
7   transactions or occurrences; and (B) any question of law or fact common to all defendants will 
8   arise in the action.”  Fed. R. Civ. P. 20(a)(2).  The upshot of these rules is that “multiple claims 
9   against a single party are fine, but Claim A against Defendant 1 should not be joined with 
10   unrelated Claim B against Defendant 2.”  George v. Smith, 507 F.3d 605, 607 (7th Cir. 2007).  In 
11   this action, Plaintiff may only seek relief for incidents that (1) arise out of the same occurrence; 
12   and (2) have a common question of law or fact common to all defendants.  The claim regarding 
13   inmate Bankhead arises out of a separate occurrence as the claim regarding CTF cell size.  Each 
14   claim involves a different set of defendants.  These two claims may not be brought in the same 
15   action.  Plaintiff may file a separate lawsuit raising his concerns about CTF cell size.  The Clerk is 
16   directed to send Plaintiff two copies of the Court’s prisoner civil rights complaint form.   
17                              CONCLUSION                                 
18        For the reasons set forth above, the Court orders as follows.    
19        1.   The Court DENIES Plaintiff’s request for leave to file the proposed third amended 
20   complaint at Dkt. No. 36, Dkt. No. 34.  The Clerk is directed to send Plaintiff two copies of the 
21   Court’s prisoner civil rights complaint form.                         
22        2.   The Court DISMISSES this action as it has already dismissed the operative 
23   complaint for failure to exhaust, Dkt. No. 33.  This dismissal is without prejudice to Plaintiff re-
24   filing a new action raising the Eighth Amendment claim raised herein after he has exhausted 
25   administrative remedies.  Judgment is entered in favor of Defendants and against Plaintiff.
