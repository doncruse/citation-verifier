13        On November 25, 2025, this Court issued an order denying the motion to compel 
14   arbitration filed by Defendants SnapCommerce Holdings, Inc. d/b/a Super.com and SnapMoney, 
15   Inc. d/b/a Super.com (together, “Super”).  ECF No. 34.  On December 16, 2025, Super filed a 
16   notice of appeal to the Ninth Circuit concerning that order.  ECF No. 37.  Before the Court is 
17   Super’s unopposed motion to stay proceedings pending its appeal.  ECF No. 38.  The Court will 
18   grant the motion.1                                                    
19        Super appealed pursuant to the Federal Arbitration Act Section 16(a), which provides that 
20   “[a]n appeal may be taken from . . . an order . . . denying a petition under section 4 of this title to 
21   order arbitration to proceed.”  ECF No. 37; 9 U.S.C. § 16(a).  Interpreting Section 16(a) in 
22   Coinbase, Inc. v. Bielski, the Supreme Court held in 2023 that “[t]he district court must stay its 
23   proceedings” while an interlocutory appeal of an order denying a motion to compel arbitration is 
24   ongoing.  599 U.S. 736, 738 (2023).  The stay applies to “those aspects of the case involved in the 
25   appeal.”  Id. at 740 (quoting Griggs v. Provident Consumer Discount Co., 459 U.S. 56, 58 (1982)).  
26   Where a decision denying a motion to compel arbitration is on appeal, “the entire case is 
27
