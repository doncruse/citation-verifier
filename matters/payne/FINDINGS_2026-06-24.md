## 🔴 Genuine problems — unsupported / misused citations

The citizen's-arrest / failure-to-charge section repeatedly cites cases for holdings they do not contain, several with **fabricated quotations**.

### Fabricated quotes (quote does not appear in the cited opinion)
- **Manzano v. State, 282 Ga. 557, 559 (2007)** — payne-11. Quoted *"A charge on justification is proper where the defendant contends that his actions were legally authorized and necessary to prevent a crime or injury."* Not in the opinion. (Manzano is a felony-murder / involuntary-manslaughter case.)
- **Durden v. State, 327 Ga. App. 173, 179 (2014)** — payne-13. Quoted *"[a]lthough the defendant requested a charge on citizen's arrest, the trial court properly charged justification, which covered the substance of his defense. A trial court is not required to give redundant instructions."* Not in the opinion. (Durden is a sword-attack aggravated-assault case.)
- **Lang v. State, 344 Ga. App. 623, 627 (2018)** — payne-14. Quoted *"[a] citizen's arrest defense is not required when a justification charge sufficiently encompasses the defendant's theory of the case."* Not in the opinion. (Lang is a gang-evidence / severance case.)

### Real cases cited for propositions they don't address (topic mismatch)
- **Manzano v. State, 282 Ga. 557 (2007)** — payne-10, payne-12 (justification as affirmative defense; rejecting a separate citizen's-arrest instruction). Opinion addresses neither.
- **Reed v. State, 291 Ga. 10 (2012)** — payne-04, payne-26 ("failure to request an unsupported jury instruction is not deficient performance"). Reed's IAC analysis never discusses jury instructions.
- **Rumph v. State, 307 Ga. 477 (2019)** — payne-07, payne-25 (IAC strategic-deference). Rumph is a Miranda/custody suppression case; no IAC analysis.
- **Hamm v. State, 294 Ga. 791 (2014)** — payne-50 (no IAC for failing to request an unsupported charge). Hamm has no IAC analysis; it is about a trial-court accomplice-corroboration charge.
- **Nelson v. State, 283 Ga. 119 (2008)** — payne-48 (citizen's-arrest instruction). Felony-murder case; no citizen's-arrest discussion.
- **Davis v. State, 269 Ga. 276, 277 (1998)** — payne-49 (deadly force in a citizen's arrest). The 1998 Davis is a felony-murder / jury-instruction case; says nothing about citizen's arrest.
- **Merneigh v. State, 242 Ga. App. 735 (2000)** — payne-63 (deadly force "subject to judicial scrutiny and jury determination"). Not the holding.
- **Virgil v. State, 227 Ga. App. 96** — payne-67 (citizen's-arrest elements / initial aggressor). Virgil is a DUI / accident-defense case.
- **Wainwright v. State, 305 Ga. 63 (2019)** — payne-65 (citizen's-arrest statutory elements). Wainwright's justification analysis is self-defense only; never mentions citizen's arrest.

### Holding cited backwards
- **Choisnet v. State, 292 Ga. 860 (2013)** — payne-79. Cited as if it affirmed a verdict on the weight/credibility of evidence. The opinion **vacated and remanded** because the trial court applied the wrong standard (used *Jackson v. Virginia* sufficiency instead of independently weighing the evidence as a "thirteenth juror"). The case is essentially the inverse of the proposition.
