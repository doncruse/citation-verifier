CASE SUMMARY:
Harrell Equipment Company appeals summary judgment dismissing its cross-claims against SunTrust Bank arising from alleged fraudulent inducement and breach of an oral agreement regarding the sale of equipment.

KEY HOLDINGS:
- Georgia's Statute of Frauds bars enforcement of an oral agreement involving a commitment to lend money, sale of goods over $500, and sale of land
- Spoliation of evidence claims require a showing of bad faith or bad conduct under federal law (Fifth Circuit standard)
- An adverse inference against a spoliator is only permitted upon showing "bad faith" or "bad conduct"

TOPICS FOUND:
- Proposition 8/9: "The Fifth Circuit permits an adverse inference against the destroyer of evidence only upon a showing of 'bad faith' or 'bad conduct.'" (p. 408-411). "Because Harrell Equipment has shown no evidence of bad faith, we find that its spoliation claim is without merit." (p. 428-429)
