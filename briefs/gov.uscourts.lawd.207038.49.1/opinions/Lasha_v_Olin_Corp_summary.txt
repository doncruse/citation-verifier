CASE SUMMARY:
A truck driver with preexisting respiratory issues was exposed to chlorine gas due to the defendant's negligence. The court held that the defendant is liable for all harm caused by the exposure and resulting aggravation of preexisting conditions.

KEY HOLDINGS:
- A defendant takes his victim as he finds him and is responsible for all natural and probable consequences of his tortious conduct
- When a defendant's tortious conduct aggravates a preexisting condition, the defendant must compensate for the full extent of the aggravation
- The defendant is liable for harm even though a normal person would not have suffered that illness or injury

TOPICS FOUND:
