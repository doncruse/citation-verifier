Cooter & Gell v. Hartmarx Corp. - Summary for Brief Assessment

TOPICS FOUND:
- Plausibility at time of filing: "whether, at the time the attorney filed the pleading or other paper, his legal argument would have appeared plausible" (p. 404, quoting Pierce v. Underwood)
- Rule 11 not a fee-shifting statute: "Rule 11 is not a fee-shifting statute" (p. 409)
- Central purpose: "to deter baseless filings in district court" (p. 395-396)
- Chilling concerns: notes concern that Rule 11 "will spawn satellite litigation and chill vigorous advocacy" (p. 395)
- Timing of sanctions: Advisory Committee intended sanctions "normally will be determined at the end of the litigation" (p. 399)
