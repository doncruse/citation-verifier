The court’s substantive analysis of a supplemental attorney fee petition for post-trial
services and costs is, of course, similar to its substantive analysis of an original fee petition.
Thus, based on case law already cited, the party seeking an award of attorneys’ fees and costs
bears the burden of proving that the request is reasonable. The court must assess the
reasonableness of the claimed attorneys’ fees and costs by using the lodestar formula. The
lodestar formula is determined by multiplying the number of hours reasonably expended on post-
trial matters by a reasonable hourly rate. The lodestar is presumed to yield a reasonable fee.
However, the court maintains the discretion to adjust the lodestar upward or downward based
upon sufficiently specific objections and other evidence, if any, submitted by the parties.
