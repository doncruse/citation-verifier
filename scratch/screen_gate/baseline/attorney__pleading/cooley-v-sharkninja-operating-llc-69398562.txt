 9                                     GENERAL ALLEGATIONS

10          11.     Prior to the incident at issue, JAMIE COOLEY received the Blender. On December

11 18, 2022, Plaintiff ordered the Cup to use in the Blender. The cup consists of a stainless-steel body

12 and a plastic top. The plastic top is affixed to the body of the Cup and is not meant to separate from

13 the body of the Cup.

14          12.     The Blender consists of a power pod, a blade assembly, and a cup. The power pod

15 contains a motor. The Blender has an on/off button and the motor is activated by attaching the cup

16 to the power pod and then pressing the power button. The blades are screwed into the Cup. The Cup

17 then fits into the power pod and is locked in via a twisting motion.

18          13.     To operate the Blender, the user adds the ingredients to the cup, screws the blade

19 assembly to the Cup, places the Cup upside down on the power pod, twists the cup into the power

20 pod until it locks, and presses the on/off and/or other mode buttons to start the motor.

21          14.     On or about January 27, 2023, Plaintiff JAMIE COOLEY was using the Blender when

22 the metal body of the Cup became unaffixed to the plastic top of the Cup. This resulted in the

23 spinning blades being exposed, the blades striking JAMIE COOLEY’s right hand, and the blades

24 severely lacerating JAMIE COOLEY’s right hand.

25          15.     At all times mentioned, all Defendants knew or had reason to know that the Blender

26 and the Cup were defective and unsafe.

 1                                         FIRST CAUSE OF ACTION

 2                         (JAMIE COOLEY AGAINST ALL DEFENDANTS)

 3                                Strict Products Liability - Design Defect

 4          16.     Plaintiffs incorporate the allegations in the preceding paragraphs, as though fully set

 5 forth herein.

 6          17.     The Blender and the Cup were defective in their design in that they did not perform

 7 as safely as an ordinary consumer would have expected it to perform when used or misused in an

 8 intended or reasonably foreseeable way.

 9          18.     The Blender and the Cup were defective in their design because the benefits were

10 outweighed by the risks they posed to consumers such as JAMIE COOLEY when used or misused

11 in an intended or reasonably foreseeable way.

12          19.     JAMIE COOLEY is informed and believes, and thereon alleges, the Blender and the

13 Cup had design defects, including but not limited to:

14                  1) The propensity of the plastic top to disengage from the metal body of the cup

15                      while in use with the Blender thereby exposing the spinning blade assembly to the

16                      user;

17                  2) The propensity that wen such disengagement occurs, the plastic top of the Cup

18                      will stay in the Blender while in use thereby exposing the spinning blade assembly

19                      to the user; and

20                  3) The blades rotate in the same direction as the cup loosening direction.

21          20.     The defective design was a substantial factor in causing JAMIE COOLEY’S damages,

22 including both economic and non-economic, including, inter alia, medical expenses, future medical

23 expenses, loss of earnings, including future earnings and loss of earning capacity, pain and suffering,

24 inconvenience, mental suffering, emotional distress, and other damages in an amount not yet

25 determined but for which California law provides a remedy.

11                                     THIRD CAUSE OF ACTION

12                        (JAMIE COOLEY AGAINST ALL DEFENDANTS)

13                              Strict Products Liability – Failure to Warn

14           25.    Plaintiffs incorporate the allegations in the preceding paragraphs, as though fully set

15 forth herein.

16           26.    All Defendants had a duty to warn JAMIE COOLEY about the risks of using the

17 Blender and the Cup.

18           27.    The Blender and the Cup had potential risks, including but not limited to the

19 propensity of the plastic top to disengage from the metal body of the cup while in use with the Blender

20 thereby exposing the spinning blade assembly to the user, that were known or knowable in light of

21 the scientific knowledge that was generally accepted in the scientific community at the time of

22 manufacture/distribution/sale. These risks presented a substantial danger when the Blender and the

23 Cup are used or misused in an intended or reasonably foreseeable way. An ordinary consumer would

24 not have recognized these risks.

25           28.    SharkNinja Operating LLC failed to adequately warn and/or instruct of these risks.

26           29.    The lack of sufficient instructions and/or warnings was a substantial factor in causing

27 JAMIE COOLEY’s harm as described in this Complaint.

 1                                   FOURTH CAUSE OF ACTION

 2                        (JAMIE COOLEY AGAINST ALL DEFENDANTS)

 3                                              Negligence

 4          30.    Plaintiffs incorporate the allegations in the preceding paragraphs, as though fully set

 5 forth herein.

 6          31.    All Defendants owed a duty of reasonable care to JAMIE COOLEY, the public, and

 7 all foreseeable users of the Blender and the Cup, when it designed, manufactured, supplied, and

 8 inspected the Blender and Cup. This duty of reasonable care required Defendants to assure that the

 9 product was not defective or unreasonably dangerous for its intended purpose and other foreseeable

10 uses.

11          32.    Defendants breached this duty of care by designing, manufacturing, supplying, and

12 inspecting the Blender and the Cup in a manner that made the device defective and unreasonably

13 dangerous for its intended and foreseeable use. This defect stems from the propensity of the plastic

14 top to disengage from the Cup’s metal body while in use with the Blender thereby exposing the

15 spinning blade assembly to the user.

16          33.    Defendants also breached their duty by failing to warn, among other things, about the

17 aforementioned propensity.

18          34.    The character of the failure of the Blender and the Cup was such that it would not

19 ordinarily occur without negligence.

20          35.    Defendants’ negligence was a substantial factor in causing JAMIE COOLEY’s harm

21 as described in this complaint.
