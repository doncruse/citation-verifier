Methodology: Haiku read each opinion and produced a structured summary (case summary, key holdings, relevant passages, topics not found). Opus then assessed each proposition using only the Haiku summary (not the full opinion text). Opinions under 20K characters were read directly by Opus (no Haiku prescreen). Ground truth (GT) assessments are from prior Opus-direct reads of the full opinions.

Observations:
1. Conservative bias is dominant: 21/24 mismatches are conservative (H→O stricter than GT). Only 3 false upgrades across 102 claims.
2. Several GT assessments appear generous in hindsight (Kettering rows 13, 17, 21; Valve rows 44, 59). The Haiku→Opus path may be producing more accurate assessments in some cases.
3. Crow Tribe pattern: 6 of 17 Valve mismatches involve Crow Tribe v. Racicot, which has a clear general holding ("Expert testimony is not proper for issues of law") that GT rated Green/Yellow when applied loosely. H→O correctly flags that the specific quoted language or application context is fabricated.
4. False upgrades are mild: All 3 are Red→Yellow or Yellow→Green, never Red→Green. Two (rows 39, 42) involve opinions that discuss related concepts but not the specific holding claimed.
5. Valve is harder: 73% match vs 81-83% for the other briefs. The Valve brief reuses the same ~10 cases across 63 claims with progressively looser application. GT appears to have been more lenient for "general principle" cites.
