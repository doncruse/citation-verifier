### Observations

1. **Classifier vs verifier mismatch:** 13/20 "likely_fake" came back VERIFIED. The keyword classifier flags citations *near* hallucination language, but many are real law the court cites while discussing the hallucination problem. The classifier needs work but that's extraction-side, not verifier-side.
2. **CalApp coverage gap:** Booth v. Allstate (198 Cal.App.3d 1357) is a real case not in CL. This is a CL data issue, not a verifier bug.
3. **Duplicate in sample:** Cohen citation appeared twice with different reporter spacing. Dedup should normalize spacing.
4. **Parsing artifact:** "In re Suday II)" has a stray parenthesis — extraction cleanup issue.
5. **Wrong RECAP match:** Mader v. Advanced Neuromodulation matched the wrong docket entirely. Verifier bug to investigate.
